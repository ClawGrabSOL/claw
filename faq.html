The Basics

General Questions

CLAW is an open-source, decentralized peer-to-peer payment network. It is the world's first AI-native blockchain — designed from the ground up to support AI agents as first-class participants. No banks. No borders. No central authority.
CLAW was conceived and built by an open-source team of developers, cryptographers, and AI researchers. The project is maintained by its community — anyone can contribute to the codebase on GitHub.
Bitcoin is digital gold — a store of value with a simple transaction layer. Ethereum introduced programmable smart contracts. CLAW combines both with a third innovation: native support for AI agents. AI can own CLAW wallets, execute smart contracts, and transact autonomously without human co-signing. CLAW also achieves sub-second finality with near-zero fees — something neither Bitcoin nor Ethereum can match natively.
Yes. 100%. All code is publicly available on GitHub under the MIT license. Anyone can read it, audit it, fork it, or contribute to it. There are no hidden components.
The total supply of CLAW is fixed at 21,000,000 — the same as Bitcoin. New CLAW is issued as block rewards to validators and decreases over time through a halving schedule. No new CLAW can ever be created beyond the cap.

Technical

Technical Questions

CLAW uses Proof of Stake (PoS). Validators stake CLAW as collateral to participate in block production. Dishonest behavior results in slashing — losing a portion of staked funds. This aligns validator incentives with network health.
CLAW achieves sub-400ms block times with immediate transaction finality. Practically speaking, your transaction confirms in under a second. Compare to Bitcoin (10 minutes) or Ethereum (~12 seconds).
Yes. CLAW is fully EVM-compatible. Solidity smart contracts deploy without modification. Existing tooling — MetaMask, Hardhat, Foundry, Ethers.js — works out of the box. Developers can port Ethereum dApps to CLAW in minutes.
AI agents on CLAW are first-class participants. They can generate and hold wallets, sign transactions using cryptographic keys, interact with smart contracts, and receive or send funds — all without any human approval step. The CLAW SDK provides native libraries for popular AI frameworks.
